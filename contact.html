Frequently Asked Questions

Quick Answers to Common Questions

Before contacting us, check if your question is answered below. Many common questions about hosting, AdSense, blogging, and affiliate marketing are covered in our FAQ.

Which web hosting provider is best for a new blog? +

The "best" hosting provider depends on your specific needs, budget, and technical expertise. For new bloggers, we generally recommend:

  • Budget-friendly options: Shared hosting plans from reputable providers (typically $3-10/month)
  • Performance-focused: Managed WordPress hosting for better speed and security
  • Scalability: Cloud hosting if you expect rapid growth

Check our hosting reviews for detailed comparisons and current recommendations.

How long does it take to get AdSense approval? +

AdSense approval typically takes 1-4 weeks, but can vary based on:

  • Website quality: Original, valuable content is essential
  • Technical requirements: Proper privacy policy, about page, contact information
  • Traffic volume: While not strictly required, some traffic helps
  • Application completeness: Accurate information and verified ownership

We have detailed guides on AdSense approval requirements that can help improve your chances.

What's the best way to start affiliate marketing? +

Starting affiliate marketing successfully involves several key steps:

  1. Choose a niche: Focus on an area you're passionate about and has market demand
  2. Create valuable content: Build a website with helpful information for your audience
  3. Select relevant products: Choose affiliate programs that match your content and audience needs
  4. Build traffic: Use SEO, social media, and email marketing to attract visitors
  5. Be transparent: Always disclose affiliate relationships as required by FTC guidelines

Our affiliate marketing guides provide step-by-step instructions.

How can I increase my website's RPM (Revenue Per Mille)? +

Improving your RPM involves several strategies:

  • Ad placement optimization: Test different ad locations and formats
  • Content quality: Higher-quality content attracts better-paying ads
  • Audience targeting: Content that attracts specific demographics often has higher RPM
  • Website speed: Faster websites have better ad viewability
  • Ad network diversification: Consider adding header bidding or alternative ad networks
  • Seasonal content: Some topics have higher advertiser demand at certain times

Try our RPM calculator tool to estimate potential earnings and identify optimization opportunities.
